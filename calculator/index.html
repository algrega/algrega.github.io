How to Use Our Percentage Calculators

Our versatile percentage calculator helps you with various calculations. Here's how to use each section and some practical examples:

1. Percentage Increase/Decrease Calculator

Use this "online tool for percent change" to find the percentage difference (increase or decrease) from an initial value to a final value. For example, if you want to know "how to calculate percentage increase" in your salary from $50,000 to $55,000, or the percentage decrease in price of an item from $100 to $80 when looking for a discount.

Example: If a stock price went from $150 to $175, enter 150 in "from" and 175 in "to". The result will show the percentage increase of your "investment percentage change".

2. "What is X% of Y?" Calculator

This calculator answers the common question: "what is X percent of Y?". It helps you find a specific percentage of a given total amount. The basic "what is X percent of Y formula" is (X/100) * Y.

Example: To find out what 20% of 150 is, enter 20 in the first box and 150 in the second. This is useful to "calculate discount percentage" (e.g., a 20% discount on an item priced at $150), or a 15% tip on a $50 bill.

3. "X is What Percent of Y?" Calculator

Use this when you have two numbers and want to find out what percentage the first number represents of the second number. It's great for comparing a part to a whole.

Example: If you answered 40 questions correctly out of a total of 50 questions on a test, enter 40 in the first box and 50 in the second. The result will tell you your percentage score. Or, if your "salary increase percentage" was $200 on a $2000 salary, you can find what percentage that increase represents.

Tips: Use tab to move to the next field. Use shift-tab to move to the previous field. Press enter to calculate.
